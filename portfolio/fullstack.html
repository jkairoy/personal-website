The Website

I personally created the website for full stack at brown and this lower half will be dedicated to discussing the public and private sides of the website

The Public Side

For the website, I chose to go with a very minimalist design, opting for flat images (which I designed in Adobe Illustrator), and chosing a sarif/sans serif font combo. I will now discuss the technical details of the visual implementation, if you would like to know more, check out the github project.
THE GALAXY
To make the galaxy effect, I created a few SVG circles and rotated them off axis (using jQuery) on separate timer loops. I then created a mask with these circles using a static SVG gradient with the typical blues and purples of space. I also made a few different images of assorted stars in Adobe Illustrator and modulated their opacities using random timers. Finally, I applied a gaussian blur to the SVG and feColorMatrix mask to reassemble the image. After this, I put on a preassembled planet SVG, added some mouse jiggle effects, and watched the very computationally taxing magic take place. This effect actually proved to be so taxing that I implemented a frame meter that checks to see if the computer can handle the animation and shuts it off in the first 5 sections if it can not.
THE ROCKET (NOW GONE)
For the rocket ship, there were three major parts to the implementation one being very noticable, and two being hardly noticeable at all. I will begin with a less noticed one, the first being the perspective trick used for the rocket window. The rocket is actually split into two different components, the window and the body. The body and the window move around the mouse at different rates to simulate perspective from the point of view of the mouse. This was implemented using Tween for movement. Not a very noticeable feature, but still fun. Next, I created the exhaust using a loop of relocating and moving opaque spheres from the location of the rocket ship. Finally, as a small easter-egg-ish feature, I implemented a mouse tracking feature that will make the exhaust become more uncontrollable and more opaque when the mouse is hovered over it. It’s likely that no-one will ever notice this, but I enjoyed it nonetheless.
THE BEAR
I felt that the bear was emblematic of the club, being a brown affiliated organization. I also felt the need to give it headphones, which naturally lead to the need to animate a slew of musical notes. This was the simplest animation to complete, essentially being a replication of the rocket exhaust but at a much slower period and with much lower animation speed. Everytime a musical note is created, its opacity is animated in a promptly animated out, leading to the smooth appearance and disappearance.

The Private Side

What the public doesn't see is that there is actually a full CMS operating on fullstackatbrown.com. I will use this section to describe the tools that we use to manage the rather large club. All of the CMS functionality is built from scratch using Postgres and node.js/express.
THE MEMBER PORTAL
Each member has access to a member portal where they are able to change their user information as well as view their current projects and access their project selection rounds.
THE ADMIN PORTAL
Administrators also have access to the admin portal, where we are able to manage and process different project proposals, tracking their statuses and adding notes where needed. The admin portal also gives users the ability to start and manage project selection rounds, project groups, and users. Much of the information managed through the admin portal is piped directly to the public side of the site, on the about and projects pages.
